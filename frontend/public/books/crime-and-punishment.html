Crime and Punishment – Fyodor Dostoevsky

First published in 1866, Crime and Punishment follows the story of Raskolnikov, a young student in Saint Petersburg, who commits a crime and faces the moral and psychological consequences. It is a profound exploration of guilt, redemption, and human nature.

About the Author

Fyodor Dostoevsky (1821–1881) was a Russian novelist, short story writer, and philosopher. He is famous for exploring deep psychological and moral themes in his novels.
